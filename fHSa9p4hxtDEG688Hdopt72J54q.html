Foods that cause headaches

Foods that cause headaches

Headache is one of the most common complaints. During one calendar year, about 90% of people suffer from headaches. Determining the cause in most cases is challenge. It is time to pay attention to one culprit of headaches, which we rarely suspect - food. Of course, genetics plays a big role here, and the greater the predisposition to headaches, the greater the impact of nutrition. Sometimes changes in the weather, a strong smell, bright light, or a menstrual cycle can cause headache. Although some of the causes of their occurrence are independent of the individual, others, such as food intake, can be controlled.

1. Alcohol.

There are opinions that the cause of headaches when drinking alcohol is that it is not clean and contains harmful impurities, or that the breakdown of alcohol in the blood is not a cause of headache. in the body, it leads to the formation of toxic products. Wine, beer, whiskey and champagne are the drinks that most often cause headaches.

2. Chocolate.

In small quantities, it is not harmful, but, for example, eating 4-5 chocolate bars can lead to severe headaches.

3. Tyramine-containing products.

Cheese, as well as some other foods such as meat and yogurt, may contain tyramine. This is a potential cause of headache. Tyramine is a breakdown product that is formed during fermentation and decomposition of products. For this reason, you should avoid stale foods with high content of this substance.

4. Citrus fruits.

Some people experience headaches when consuming fruits that contain, for example, octopamine, present in bitter orange.

Others have an intolerance to fruit acids, which are present in large quantities available in oranges, lemons, and grapefruits.

5. Cold foods.

Consuming excessively cold foods, such as ice cream, can affect the nerve endings in the mouth and cause headaches.

6. Products containing preservatives and dyes.

Some products contain dyes and preservatives that cause headaches. It is believed that this is due to the expansion of blood vessels. Unlike the headache caused by tyramine, here it is felt bilaterally and disappears after stopping consumption of a product containing this harmful ingredient.

7. Msg.

Glutamate is also one of the causes of headaches. It used to be called "Chinese restaurant syndrome". Manifests itself a couple of hours after eating Chinese food. Sometimes it is accompanied by dizziness and abdominal pain.

Food consumption trigger foods can be crucial for preventing or reducing headaches. Methods that can help reduce headaches are meditation and training in breathing techniques.
