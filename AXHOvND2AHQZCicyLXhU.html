Toxic hepatitis-damage to the liver by various substances

Toxic hepatitis is liver damage caused by various chemicals. Almost all harmful elements that enter the bloodstream are neutralized by the liver. In it they are they are converted into products that are excreted in the urine or bile. However, many chemicals, such as medications, alcohol, and various industrial products, can have toxic effects on the liver.

What are the causes of toxic hepatitis?

Toxic hepatitis can be caused by for the following reasons:

Medicines.

Depending on the dose, duration of administration, and state of the body, almost all medications can cause liver damage. Some antibiotics, anti-inflammatory aides, cytostatics, statins, antiepileptic aides, and many other agents they provoke the appearance of drug-induced hepatitis. In combination with alcohol, they are especially dangerous.

Alcohol.

It is known that alcohol abuse can lead to alcoholic hepatitis. The degree of damage depends on the duration, amount of alcohol consumed, as well as on the individual sensitivity to it.

Herbs and nutritional supplements.

Some herbs and supplements are not completely harmless and can damage the liver. People with liver disease should consult their doctor before using any herbal supplements.

Chemical assistants.

Row industrial chemicals-pesticides can cause liver damage.

Poisonous mushroom.

Poisoning with poisonous mushrooms (red fly agaric, white fly agaric) damages the liver.

Toxic hepatitis is also caused by drugs and other toxic substances.

What are the symptoms of toxic hepatitis?

In some cases, toxic hepatitis develops within a few hours or days, and in others it takes months before the appearance of complaints.

Some of the most common symptoms of toxic hepatitis:

- yellowing of the skin and mucous membranes;

- dimout can;

- vomiting;

- abdominal pain;

- high temperature;

- drowsiness, change of consciousness;

- fatigue, anorexia;

- skin eruption;

- loose stool.

In most cases, toxic hepatitis resolves itself after exposure is stopped it is not a toxic substance, but in severe cases, serious complications can occur, such as cirrhosis of the liver and liver failure, which is a life-threatening condition.

How is toxic hepatitis diagnosed?

The following tests and procedures help diagnose toxic hepatitis:

1. Anamnesis and physical examination - the doctor asks the patient about medications taken, the presence of harmful chemicals in the workplace, about possible excessive use of mushrooms, alcohol.

2. Laboratory tests - liver enzymes can show whether there is a violation of its function.

3. Ultrasound examination, computed tomography.

4. A tissue biopsy of the liver - this test can confirm the diagnosis of toxic hepatitis. A small piece of liver is extracted with a needle and examined under a microscope.

How to treat toxic hepatitis?

The first and most important task is to stop it exposure to a toxic substance that caused the disease. Gastric lavage may be performed or artificial vomiting may be induced to remove poisons.

After that, bed rest is recommended. The diet should be sparing for the liver, vitamins and hepatoprotectors are used. Treatment is performed only under the supervision of under the supervision of a specialist doctor. In most cases, there is a complete recovery of the patient.

Rarely, only after very serious intoxication, a liver transplant may be required.
