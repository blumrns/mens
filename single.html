Influence of alcohol on men's and women's health

Although drinking one or two glasses of low-strength alcohol is perfectly normal from time to time, systemic binge drinking can put your health at risk men.

The health effects of alcohol are largely the same for men and women, but there are conditions that are more specific to men and others that are specific only to women.

The bodies of men and women metabolize alcohol differently. Men have a higher body weight than women, and therefore, they have more tissues to absorb alcohol. In addition, alcohol dehydrogenase, an enzyme that breaks down alcohol, is three times more common in men than in women. Men have a lower fat-to-water ratio than women, which means that they are more able to dilute alcohol in the body. Therefore, when men and women they drink the same amount of alcohol, and men do not get as high a concentration of alcohol in their blood as women.

However, men tend to experience more overall body harm associated with alcohol than women, because on average they drink more alcohol.

Alcohol impairs the ability to make the right decisions and respond appropriately, and men are more likely to take risks during drinking sprees. All this explains why men have an increased rate of alcohol-related deaths and hospitalizations than women.

Alcohol poisoning is more common in men, they often make rash actions after intoxications. Men are more likely than women to be aggressive, have risky sex, have accidents, or even commit suicide when drinking alcohol.

Long-term excessive alcohol consumption has serious consequences for both physical and mental health.

Alcoholic hepatitis and cirrhosis of the liver are two main problems for men's health. Men with long-term alcohol dependence are more likely to have severe conditions with high mortality. These are, for example, cancers of the oral cavity, esophagus, colon, liver.

Long-term alcohol use also promotes the development of myocardial infarction, gout, can worsen the condition of patients with psoriasis, rosacea, diabetes and others.

Alcohol abuse leads to many mental health problems, such as anxiety, depression, changeable behavior, aggression, memory changes, and personality disorders, dependence.

Excessive alcohol consumption can affect testicular function and male hormone production. When this happens, men can experience erectile dysfunction (impotence) as well as infertility.

Alcohol depresses the Central nervous system and, therefore, makes it difficult to achieve and maintain a healthy weight. maintaining an erection. In addition, alcohol reduces the production of male hormone, which leads to a decrease in the male sexual organ and a deterioration in the number and quality of spermatozoa.

The decline in male fertility may be partly explained by the fact that excessive alcohol consumption interferes with absorption certain vitamins necessary for sperm production.
